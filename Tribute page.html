ALLAMA IQBAL

1877 - 1938

Poet Of The East

Mohammad Iqbal (09 Novembar 1877 â€“ 21 April 1938) a descendant of a Kashmiri Brahmin family that had embraced Islam in the seventeenth century, was born and settled in Sialkot. After a traditional education in Arabic, Persian, and Urdu, he was exposed to a liberal education that defined the contours of his thought and his poetry during the entire period of his life. Beginning his educational career at the Scottish Mission School, he went on to acquire his M. A. in Philosophy, before joining Trinity College, and later earning the degree of Bar-at-Law. He furthered his education by getting the degree of doctorate from Germany on The Development of Metaphysics in Persia. He worked in different capacities at different points of time; he taught philosophy, practised law, got involved in politics, and also attended the second Round Table Conference. Even while he favoured the idea of the creation of Pakistan and is venerated there as the national poet, he wrote the famous patriotic song that celebrates the greatness of India. About The Legend:

  • Education

    Studied in Europe, including Cambridge University, where he earned a degree in philosophy. Earned a doctorate from the University of Munich in Germany.

  • Philosophical Contributions

    Developed the concept of Khudi (selfhood) and emphasized the importance of self-realization. Advocated for the revival of Islamic spirituality and philosophical thought. Proposed the idea of a Muslim state in the Indian subcontinent.

  • Literary Work

    Renowned poet in the Urdu and Persian languages. Wrote poetry collections such as "Bang-e-Dra," "Asrar-e-Khudi," and "Bal-e-Jibril." His poetry reflects themes of spirituality, self-awareness, and the awakening of the Muslim community.

  • Political Involvement

    Played a key role in inspiring the Pakistan Movement through his vision of a separate Muslim state. Active in the All-India Muslim League and supported the idea of an independent Muslim state.

  • Recognition and Titles

    Awarded the title of "Sir" by the British Crown in 1922. Recognized as (The Thinker of Pakistan) and (The Thinker of Pakistan) (The Poet of the East).

  • Legacy

    Regarded as one of the most important figures in Urdu literature and Islamic philosophy National poet of Pakistan. His poetry and philosophical ideas continue to inspire thinkers, poets, and political leaders.

  • Death

    Allama Iqbal passed away on April 21, 1938, in Lahore, British India (now in Pakistan).
